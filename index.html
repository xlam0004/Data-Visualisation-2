United Kingdom is one of the most popular countries for international students to pursue higher education. The country has a rich history of higher-education and most of the universities here are well-known all over the world. The vibrant and multicultural society is also one of the main keys that attract foreign student to study abroad. With that, this visualization gives information about the top universities in the United Kingdom for Year 2022 such as the ranking of each university, expected expenses, student population statistics and factors that affect the student enrolment.

Student Population Statistics Across Different States in the United Kingdom

The overall percentage of international student in each state of the United Kingdom can be observed in the donut chart below. London is one of the states that most of the international student will consider to go when pursuing higher education due to the quality of education. This can be observed in the ranking above where many top universities are located in London. The range dot plot on the right shows the ratio of female and male in each universities. According to Higher Education Policy Institute (2020), it is observe that there are still a significant gender gap between courses where women are less likely to study STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics) subjects. This implies the interesting fact that Bishop Grosseteste University, located in East of England, has the largest gender gap where the female:male ratio is 80:20. This is because most of the courses that Bishop Grosseteste University offers are Art, Business and Education major.

Factors That Can Affect Student Enrolment

According to the bubble chart below, it is observed that most of the top universities in the United Kingdom have the IELTS Score requirement of 4.5 and 5.5. We can also observe that there is no significant change in the percentage of internationa student when the IELTS score changes. When the IELTS score requirement is 4.0, the percentage of international student ranges from 30% to 47% while the percentage of the international student ranges from 39% to 72% when IELTS score requirement is 6.5. Hence, we say that IELTS score requirement may not be a factor that affect the student enrolment. Besides, the scatter plot on the right shows that the number of years a university operates has little to no correlation to the number of student enrolment. However, an interesting fact is that most of the universities are founded in year 1992 which is 30 years ago. This is due to the abolition of binary divide between universities and polytechnics where all polytechnics and Scottish central institutions become universities under the Further and Higher Education Act 1992.
